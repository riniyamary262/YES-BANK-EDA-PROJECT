This project conducts a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on Yes Bank's stock prices spanning from July 2005 to November 2020. The dataset comprises 185 monthly records with five key variables: Date, Open, High, Low, and Close prices. The primary objective is to uncover hidden patterns, trends, and relationships within the stock's historical performance to derive actionable business insights.

The analysis reveals a fascinating journey of Yes Bank's stock. Starting at approximately ₹12 in 2005, the stock demonstrated steady growth over the years, reaching its all-time peak of ₹384 in August 2017. However, the most dramatic story unfolds post-2017 when the bank faced significant challenges, leading to a catastrophic crash. By July 2020, the stock plummeted to an alarming low of ₹11.95, nearly wiping out all the gains accumulated over 15 years.

The EDA process involves multiple stages: data cleaning, feature engineering, univariate analysis, bivariate analysis, and multivariate analysis. We created derived features including daily returns (percentage change), 6-month rolling volatility, price range (High-Low), and opening gaps (Open - Previous Close) to gain deeper insights into price behavior.

The univariate analysis reveals that the closing prices exhibit a right-skewed distribution, with most values concentrated between ₹0-100. The average closing price stands at approximately ₹94, while the median is much lower at ₹52, indicating the presence of extreme high values pulling the mean upward. Daily returns are generally centered around zero with occasional extreme positive and negative movements, reflecting the stock's volatile nature.

Bivariate analysis uncovers strong positive correlations between Open, High, Low, and Close prices (correlation coefficients > 0.95), suggesting these variables move in near-perfect synchronization. However, the correlation between daily returns and price levels is weak, indicating that percentage changes are not dependent on the absolute price level. Interestingly, higher stock prices tend to be associated with wider price ranges, suggesting increased trading activity during high price periods.

The multivariate analysis through pair plots and correlation heatmaps confirms that all price variables are highly intercorrelated, which has important implications for predictive modeling. The analysis also reveals seasonal patterns, with certain months showing historically higher or lower average returns, though these patterns are not consistently strong enough to serve as reliable trading signals.

Time series analysis shows clear cyclical patterns with periods of steady appreciation followed by sharp corrections. The addition of moving averages (6-month and 12-month) helps identify major trend reversals, particularly the crossover points that preceded significant market movements. The 6-month moving average crossing below the 12-month moving average in early 2019 served as a bearish signal that correctly predicted the subsequent crash.

This comprehensive analysis provides investors and stakeholders with valuable insights into Yes Bank's price behavior, volatility patterns, and risk characteristics. The findings support better-informed investment decisions, improved risk management strategies, and more accurate price prediction models. The project ultimately demonstrates the power of EDA in transforming raw financial data into strategic business intelligence.
